1983

The internet is created on 1 January 1983. While the invention of the internet cannot be credited to one person the modern internet was developed from ARPANET’s adoption communications model created by Robert Kahn and Vinton Cerf.

1984

Two french students develop and release MAD(Multi-Access Dungeon), this is the first game to be available on the world wide web. It ran on BITNET, a cooperative worldwide university network. It was a text-based dungeon game that ran on the central MAD server. It was up for two years until the administrators took it down after becoming fearful of how popular it was.

1986

SGI Dogfight is the first game to use the Internet Protocol, it was limited to a single network. The game was so intensive that it was limited and could only be played over the ethernet with other players, it wasn’t capable of passing through a router.

1989

IP Multicast is developed. This allows data to be communicated from a server to a set of clients who want the data. Clients can enter and leave the communication dynamically.

1990

SEGA capitalizes on IP Multicast by releasing Mega net. This provided the first online multiplayer game. It utilized dial-up internet and while multiple titles were available to play with friends online the service only lasted for a year.

1994

The first pay-to-play game for the internet is released. Avalon MUD was a multiplayer RPG. Avalon introduced a series of innovations to the genre that relied on multiplayer such as banking, trading, and real estate.

1996

Windows 95 is released online with affordable Ethernet cards. These make it far easier to access the internet and is huge for the online gaming industry.

1996

The flash player is released. Its purpose was to make web graphics and animations easily.

2000

Flash got its programming language and making games with it became possible. The ActionScript language was an enhanced superset of ECMAScript with a Java-style class model.

2020

After nearly 20 years of allowing anyone to make games flash was discontinued. Its decline in popularity can largely be attributed to creators either switching to bigger engines like Unity or Unreal to make mobile and console games.
